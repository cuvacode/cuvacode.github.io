A home workplace away from workplace.

Traditional 9-to-5 jobs are not for everybody. I learned that lesson firsthand after jumping from position to position, wondering why I couldn't keep one for longer than 18 months.

After taking the leap and deciding to go off on my own, I quickly found out that there was a world of things I was missing by renting out my brain for someone else's agenda.

Why has coworking sucked in the past? No acceleration. Not resource. Just a desk.

Welcome to the Code Shop

What's included with the space?

Private Patio + BBQ
Bring your laptop out on the patio and bring some weiners to toss on the grill.
Security
Leave your belongings or take them with you. We have a lockitron system so we know who is in and who is out at all times.

Why should I come?

Accountability
It's easy to work from home, but it isn't always easy to hold yourself accountable. Be around other people who will help make sure you get shit done.
Collaboration
Meet other devs + get help + gain trust
Self-Improvement
Learn about starting your own business, productizing, code katas, be the worst in the room.
Work Referrals
Get project/client leads
Community
Monthly Meetups in cellar/patio

What makes you so different?

In the National Park
Peninsula is the only city located inside of the National PArk. It's awesome and feels like a vacation town. Get out of the city and get outside.
Code Campfire
Once a month, we host a campfire cookout where you can come, talk shop, or just hang out and meet people.
Bicycle Storage
Bring your bike once and keep it down here. When the weather gets nice, you can take it for a spin whenever you want on the best roads and trails in Northeast Ohio.
Friday Trailblazers
Join us on a walk on the Towpath Trail for an hour every Friday before work to get out and move while recapping your week and planning for next.

Are there membership fees?

Money is not the objective.

Once a month, you should factor in contributing one or more of the following:

  • Write an article
  • Record a video tutorial
  • Prep a talk
  • Lead a workshop/class
  • Submit pull requests to an organization project
  • 10 hours of pair programming availability
  • Supply beer and coffee

If none of these work for you, you can alternatively contribute $150 a month as a membership fee.
